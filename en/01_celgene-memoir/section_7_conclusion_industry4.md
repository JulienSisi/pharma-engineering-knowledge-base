## 7.2 What experience confirms that literature predicts

The literature review conducted via Consensus reveals remarkable convergence between practices developed in the field and recommendations from academic and industrial research.

Three convergences deserve to be highlighted:

**Criticality as organizing principle** — Classification of assets by RPN (Risk Priority Number) and the differentiation of maintenance strategies that follows is the common thread throughout all literature on pharmaceutical maintenance. This is not simply good practice: it is the prerequisite for rational use of resources in a constrained environment.

**Data as strategic asset** — Oracle CMMS is not a ticketing tool. It is a database that, when properly fed, becomes a source of operational intelligence enabling the shift from reactive to predictive management. This dimension is systematically underexploited in organizations that have not made field data quality a managerial priority.

**Coordination as enduring competency** — Regardless of the level of technological sophistication achieved, the ability to make teams with partially divergent objectives work together remains an irreplaceable human skill.

## 7.3 Industry 4.0 & predictive pharmaceutical maintenance

The fourth industrial revolution is progressively transforming pharmaceutical maintenance. Its key components in pharma context:

### Internet of Things (IoT) & connected sensors

Deployment of sensors on process equipment enables continuous data collection (temperature, vibration, pressure, motor current) without human intervention. This data feeds anomaly detection algorithms that signal degradation before it manifests as failure.

```
IoT Sensor → Real-time data → ML Algorithm → 
Predictive alert → Conditional WO → Targeted intervention
```

### Digital Twins

Digital twins allow simulation of equipment behavior under real operating conditions, optimization of preventive maintenance intervals, and testing of parameter modifications without production risk.

### Machine Learning applied to maintenance

ML models trained on Oracle historical data (failures, MTBF, operating conditions) can generate failure predictions with precision levels that exceed traditional heuristic approaches.

## 7.4 Specific challenges of digital transition in pharma

Literature identifies several barriers specific to the pharmaceutical sector:

- **Regulatory data integrity**: any data generated by automated systems must satisfy ALCOA requirements and be defensible during FDA/SwissMedic audits
- **Computer systems validation** (21 CFR Part 11): new IoT/ML tools must be validated as GMP computerized systems
- **Change Control**: introduction of new monitoring technologies may impact qualification of affected equipment
- **Skills**: maintenance technician profiles evolve toward data literacy skills in addition to traditional technical competencies

## 7.5 Coordination as enduring competency

Technological evolution does not diminish — it amplifies — the importance of inter-departmental coordination. Data generated by IoT systems is shared between Engineering, Quality and Operations. Their interpretation and resulting decisions require **organizational alignment** that only effective human coordination can guarantee.

## 7.6 Organizational maturity & benchmarking

### Maintenance maturity models

Chikwendu et al. (2020) and Drennen (2020) articulate their model around **ten maturity factors**:

```
1. Maintenance culture           6. CMMS & digital tooling
2. Maintenance policy           7. Spare parts management
3. Performance management       8. Documentation standardization
4. Failure analysis            9. Skills development
5. Planning & scheduling       10. Leadership & strategic management
```

### The LSM model: Lean Smart Maintenance

Maier, Schmiedbauer & Biedermann (2020; 2021) developed the **Lean Smart Maintenance Maturity Model (LSM)**, which articulates efficiency (Lean) and digital integration (Smart):

| LSM Domain | Level achieved | Evolution lever identified |
|---|---|---|
| Culture | Proactive — maintenance valued, integrated into QMS | Strengthen data-driven culture |
| Assets strategy | Advanced — criticality matrix, risk-based planning | Toward predictive maintenance |
| Process organization | Structured — multi-department coordination, CAPA | Automate eQMS workflows |
| Data & technology | Intermediate — Oracle CMMS, dashboards | Digital twins, IoT sensors |
| Knowledge management | Solid — SOPs, WPs, training, lessons learned | Systematic capitalization |

## 7.7 Prospective synthesis

The trajectory traced by this thesis is not a straight line toward a predetermined technological destination. It is continuous progress on several simultaneous fronts:

- **Technical**: growing mastery of monitoring and predictive analysis technologies
- **Regulatory**: continuous adaptation to evolving GMP framework (ICH Q10, data integrity)
- **Organizational**: development of coordination capabilities in increasingly complex environments
- **Strategic**: positioning maintenance as a value-creating function, not just cost manager

Evolution toward Industry 4.0 will increase the quantity and quality of data available to make these decisions. It will not replace judgment, field experience, and the ability to make teams with sometimes divergent objectives work together — all dimensions that this professional journey has allowed to develop and exercise in a context of high regulatory requirements.

> *"Total Productive Maintenance and Total Quality Management together have a significant positive impact on operational performance."*  
> — Modgil & Sharma (2016)
