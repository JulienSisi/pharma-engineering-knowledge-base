# Section 5 — Product Context: Lénalidomide, Thalidomide & Aprémilast

> **Methodological note:** This section relies exclusively on public data from scientific literature and regulatory publications. No proprietary or confidential information relating to the production site is disclosed.

## 5.1 Lénalidomide (Revlimid®)

### Public pharmacological profile

Lénalidomide is a second-generation immunomodulatory agent (IMiD), a structural derivative of thalidomide. It is indicated for the treatment of multiple myeloma and certain myelodysplastic syndromes. Its classification as a **REMS drug** (Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategy) in the United States imposes particularly strict distribution and traceability constraints.

### Maintenance implications

- **Containment**: Manufacturing an IMiD in an API environment requires reinforced containment measures and rigorous effluent management.
- **Validated cleaning**: Cleaning procedures (CIP — Cleaning In Place or validated manual cleaning) must be qualified and their residue limits justified. Maintenance of cleaning equipment is directly critical.
- **Equipment qualification**: All equipment coming into contact with the product or its intermediates is subject to the IQ/OQ/PQ cycle and periodic qualification reviews (Annual Product Review).

## 5.2 Thalidomide (Thalomid®)

### Public pharmacological profile

Thalidomide, the first historical IMiD, remains used in specific hematological indications (multiple myeloma in combination). Its regulatory history has contributed to shaping modern cGMP requirements for traceability and risk management.

### Maintenance implications

Thalidomide maintenance constraints are similar to those of lénalidomide from a GMP perspective, with particular emphasis on:
- Analytical validation of residue detection methods (HPLCs, spectroscopies)
- Maintenance of containment and aspiration systems
- Document management of deviations related to out-of-specification measuring equipment

## 5.3 Aprémilast (Otezla®)

### Public pharmacological profile

Aprémilast is a phosphodiesterase 4 (PDE4) inhibitor, indicated for the treatment of psoriasis and psoriatic arthritis. Unlike IMiDs, it presents a different risk profile that is reflected in its manufacturing requirements.

### Transition to continuous manufacturing

Recent literature (Burcham et al., 2018; Müller et al., 2021) documents the growing interest in **continuous API manufacturing** (Continuous Manufacturing), of which aprémilast is a studied candidate. This transition to continuous processes implies different maintenance requirements from batch processes:

- **Real-time monitoring** of process parameters (PAT — Process Analytical Technology)
- **Predictive maintenance** made possible by continuous generation of process data
- **Qualification of continuous equipment** — regulatory framework still maturing

## 5.4 Cross-cutting implications for maintenance strategy

The diversity of profiles of these three molecules — two IMiDs in batch process with reinforced containment, one PDE4 inhibitor transitioning toward continuous manufacturing — justifies a **differentiated maintenance approach by line and by equipment**.

Degraded but undetected equipment directly becomes a **potential regulatory deviation**. This is precisely the logic underlying the criticality matrix and the Oracle CMMS monitoring system described in Section 2.
