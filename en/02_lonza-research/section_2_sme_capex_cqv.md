# Section 2 — SME in CAPEX projects: CQV Framework in biologics environment

## 2.1 The SME role in the CAPEX equipment lifecycle

The Subject Matter Expert (SME) for equipment in a CAPEX project is not a technical observer — they are the critical interface between project engineering teams, production, quality, and suppliers. Their intervention covers the entire CQV cycle:

```
CAPEX Project Lifecycle — SME Touchpoints

Phase DESIGN          → Technical specifications (URS)
Phase PROCUREMENT     → Supplier selection, FAT
Phase INSTALLATION    → Implementation supervision, IQ
Phase COMMISSIONING   → Functional verification, C&Q
Phase QUALIFICATION   → OQ/PQ, protocols, reports
Phase PRODUCTION      → Handover, ramp-up support
```

## 2.2 URS — User Requirement Specification

The URS document is the starting point of any qualification. It translates process needs into formal technical requirements:

- **Functional requirements**: capabilities, operating ranges, accuracy
- **GMP requirements**: product contact materials, CIP/SIP cleanability, required DQ/IQ/OQ/PQ
- **SHE requirements**: HPAPI containment, operator safety
- **Utilities requirements**: electrical supply, fluids, HVAC
- **Documentation requirements**: data integrity, audit trail, 21 CFR Part 11

## 2.3 FAT / SAT — Factory & Site Acceptance Tests

The FAT (Factory Acceptance Test) and SAT (Site Acceptance Test) are critical stages that the SME co-pilots with the supplier:

| Test | Location | Objective | SME Role |
|------|----------|-----------|----------|
| DQ | Office/Supplier | Verify that design meets URS | Review documentation |
| FAT | Supplier factory | Verify equipment before shipment | Supervise functional tests |
| SAT | Visp site | Verify after installation | Co-sign results |
| IQ | Visp site | Installation per specs | Protocol author or reviewer |
| OQ | Visp site | Operation within limits | Protocol author or reviewer |
| PQ | Visp site | Performance under real conditions | Protocol author or reviewer |

## 2.4 C&Q — Commissioning & Qualification

The ISPE GAMP framework defines the boundary between commissioning (engineering functional verification) and qualification (formal GMP proof). In practice, good engineering during the commissioning phase is the condition for effective and surprise-free qualification.

The literature (Calnan et al., 2017) emphasizes that deficiencies in the early phases of the project (incomplete URS, insufficient FAT) systematically manifest as deviations and delays in the OQ/PQ phase — a major hidden cost in biologics CAPEX projects.
