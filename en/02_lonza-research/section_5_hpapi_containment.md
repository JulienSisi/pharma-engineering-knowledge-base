# Section 5 — HPAPI Containment Engineering

## 5.1 What is an HPAPI?

An **HPAPI** (Highly Potent Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient) is an API whose therapeutic dose is less than 1 mg/kg body weight, or whose occupational exposure limit (OEL) is less than 10 µg/m³ air (8h TWA). HPAPI manufacturing requires stringent protective measures for operators and the environment.

Lonza Visp is a major player in HPAPI manufacturing, particularly for **Antibody-Drug Conjugates (ADCs)** — oncology therapies that combine a monoclonal antibody (biologics portion) with an HPAPI payload (extremely potent chemical portion).

## 5.2 Banding system — HPAPI Classification

```
Band 1  OEL > 1000 µg/m³    → Standard measures
Band 2  OEL 100-1000 µg/m³  → Reinforced measures (gloves, respirator)
Band 3  OEL 10-100 µg/m³    → Closed enclosures, dedicated room
Band 4  OEL 1-10 µg/m³      → Isolator containment, negative pressures
Band 5  OEL < 1 µg/m³       → High-performance isolators, SMEPAC required
```

ADCs can reach Band 4-5 for their payload — requiring some of the most demanding containment infrastructures in the industry.

## 5.3 Containment equipment and their maintenance

Maintenance of HPAPI equipment is a high-risk activity requiring specific procedures:

| Equipment | Maintenance risk | Key procedure |
|-----------|------------------|---------------|
| Isolators | Leak during intervention | Prior chemical decontamination, full PPE |
| HVAC/extraction systems | HEPA filter contamination | Bag-in/bag-out procedure, integrity testing |
| Seals & gaskets | Wear → exposure | Reinforced PM frequency, check before each batch |
| Weighing systems | Surface contamination | Wipe testing post-cleaning |

## 5.4 HPAPI environmental monitoring

Monitoring HPAPI contamination in production areas requires:
- Regular wipe testing of surfaces (post-cleaning and periodic)
- Air monitoring (real-time sensors or periodic sampling)
- Systematic recording in the environmental monitoring system
- Correlation with maintenance data (recent interventions, equipment condition)

## 5.5 Link to the I&U Engineer role

For the equipment engineer in an HPAPI environment, the implications are direct:
- Any intervention on equipment in an HPAPI zone requires a prior risk assessment
- Maintenance WOs include PPE and decontamination instructions
- Equipment qualification integrates containment aspects
- Collaboration with the SHE team is systematic
