# Section 4 — GMP Utilities Biologics: CIP/SIP, WFI & Clean Steam

## 4.1 Biologics utilities: critical process infrastructure

In biologics manufacturing, utilities are not simple service fluids — they are **process inputs** whose quality directly determines product compliance. This reality fundamentally distinguishes the biologics context from the small molecule API context.

### Critical utilities hierarchy

```
WFI (Water for Injection)          → Direct product contact / cleaning
Pure Steam / Clean Steam           → Sterilization, SIP
Purified Water                     → Solution preparation, cleaning
Pharmaceutical grade compressed air → Actuation, drying, pressurization
Inert gases (N2, CO2)              → Sparging, bioreactor pressurization
HVAC - Air classification          → Cleanroom environment
```

## 4.2 WFI — Water for Injection

WFI is produced by distillation (traditional method) or by reverse osmosis + CEDI (emerging method accepted by EMA since 2017). Its maintenance requirements are among the most critical on site:

- **Continuous quality monitoring**: conductivity, TOC, endotoxins, bioburden
- **Distribution loops**: temperature maintenance (80°C in hot loop) or turbulent flow velocity to prevent biofilm formation
- **Loop qualification**: complete validation of distribution system including points of use

WFI loop degradation manifests insidiously through progressive bioburden increase — without clear alarm. Preventive maintenance (inspections, scheduled sanitizations) is the only lever to prevent non-compliance.

## 4.3 CIP/SIP — Cleaning & Sterilization In Place

CIP (cleaning) and SIP (steam sterilization) systems are the guardians of equipment biological integrity between batches.

**Critical CIP/SIP maintenance:**
- Spray nozzle verification (clogging → insufficient cleaning coverage)
- Circuit integrity (seals, valves → leaks → reduced efficiency)
- Cycle parameter validation (temperature, concentration, contact time)
- Recording of each cycle in the recipe management system

The slightest undetected failure can compromise an entire batch's integrity — with major regulatory and economic consequences.

## 4.4 Transition from Celgene context

At Celgene, the managed utilities (compressed air, purified water, basic HVAC) operated in a small molecule context. The gap to master for the Visp biologics context concerns:

- Biological criticality of WFI and distribution loops
- Automated CIP/SIP systems and their qualification
- Sterile steam management (steam purity, validations)
- Utilities / bioreactor integration (feeds, harvest)
